Dr. Shini Somara is:

Shini takes cutting-edge science and translates it into warm-hearted, emotionally provoking and exciting TV. Her work has most recently been seen on Al Jazeera America and BBC1 in the UK. Shini also reports for networks such as BBC World News, BBC America, Sky and Discovery. She began her career as a mechanical engineer, completing two engineering degrees at Brunel University in London by the age of 24 years old. Her first degree was a BEng(Hons), which led her to discover her passion for 'visualising the invisible', which she studies at doctoral level, earning her an Engineering Doctorate in Computational Fluid Dynamics or CFD. CFD allows engineers to visualise (on a computer screen) how any gases or liquids flow. CFD illustrates how air flows over aircraft wings or Formula1 cars, for example. Shini loves exploring at the cutting edge of science. She's fascinated by pioneering technology and innovation and enjoys unravelling how it all works. Shini has always had a natural talent for communicating science, which she initiated by delivering talks in schools, colleges and universities. She has always been inspired by her mechanical engineering father, and hopes to pass on her enthusiasm for STEM*. Through her own career experiences, Shini aims to encourage and empower students to follow their own passions and interests. To build her knowledge and experience in communicating science on a mass scale, Shini also gained skills behind the camera working for a variety of independent production companies. Working for companies such as Oxford Scientific Films, Windfall Films and RGB Digital taught her skills in researching, producing and post-production, which have proved invaluable to her on-screen presence.
